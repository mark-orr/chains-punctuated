ORGANIZATION

The files to reproduce the results are organized in three folders:

1. "code": contains a stata do file for each result figure source data and table in the main text, supplementary information (SI), and extended data (ED), as well as a "_rundirectory.do" file that describes and executes all the do files.

2. "data": the "input" subdirectory contains the quantitative and interview datasets (in stata dta and in csv), called by the do files in (1) to produce the results, and the "figures_source_data" subdirectory contains stata dta and corresponding csv files created by the do files in (1) in the process of generating the figures, which are saved as figure source data.

3. "results": contains results in the main text, SI, and ED generated by the code in (1). It also contains stata markdown files that output into a word document each table generated by the code in (1).



EXECUTION OF CODE

To reproduce the results, follow these steps:

1. Open "code/_rundirectory.do"

2. Type in your directory path into line 12: global project // ** TYPE PROJECT DIRECTORY HERE **

3. Run "code/_rundirectory.do"

This will run each do file in the "code" directory.



DESCRIPTION OF QUANTITATIVE DATASET "cooperation_quant.dta" AND DICTIONARY OF VARIABLES

loancycle         		
Loan cycle number for the group (i.e. 1, 2, 3…).                  

schdnr	         		
Scheduled payment number within loan cycle (i.e. 1, 2, 3…).                  

lnamtmemm         		
Loan amount per member in millions of SLL.                  

nrterms         		
Number of terms (months) the loan is scheduled for.                  

schddateyear         		
The year of the scheduled monthly payment.                  

seasondummy         		
Dummy variable for rainy season, 1 = rainy (June to Nov). Affects the business cycles in Sierra Leone.                  

contrate         		
The Financial Contribution Rate is the percentage of the monthly scheduled amount paid by the group within 30 days. It ranges from 0 to 100.                   

effortrate
The Cooperative Effort Rate is based on the timeliness of the group’s preliminary payment which may be partial or full for each month. It ranges from 0 to 100.

repayq
Repayment Quotient. schdnr/nrterms. Gives how a scheduled repayment fits in with the loan's duration. For example, scheduled payment 6 of a 6 month loan would have repayq value of 1.  But scheduled payment 6 of a 12 month loan would have repayq value of 0.5.

repayq6
Payments are assigned an index 1-6 based on repayq. Essentially it is a standardized within-loan repayment number. Bin sizes are simply 1/6. If a repayq falls in that bin, it gets the number.  Therefore, loans with more than 6 scheduled payments will have more than 1 scheduled payment assigned to some bins. Constructed to match loans with different durations to a standard 6 payment schedule for visualization purposes.

groupsize
Number of members in the group.

propfemalei
Proportion of group members female. Imputation using mean substitution.

propfemale
Proportion of group members female.

propmarriedi
Proportion of group members married. Imputation using mean substitution. 

propmarried
Proportion of group members married.

avgchildi
Average number of children of group members. Imputation using mean substitution. 

avgchild
Average number of children of group members. 

avgmthsalesmi
Average Monthly Sales of group members. In millions of SLL. Imputation using mean substitution.

avgmthsalesm 
Average Monthly Sales of group members. In millions of SLL.  

sdmthsalesmi
Standard Deviation of Monthly Sales across group members. In millions of SLL. Imputation using mean substitution. 

sdmthsalesm
Standard Deviation of Monthly Sales across group members. In millions of SLL. 

avgbusequitymi
Average Business Equity of group members. In millions of SLL. Imputation using mean substitution.  

avgbusequitym
Average Business Equity of group members. In millions of SLL.

sdbusequitymi
Standard Deviation of Business Equity across group members. In millions of SLL. Imputation using mean substitution. 
  
sdbusequitym
Standard Deviation of Business Equity across group members. In millions of SLL. 

btdiversityzi
Business Type Diversity Z-score. Calculated as the number of different business types within a group divided by the number of group members. The values are normalized with a mean of zero and a standard deviation of one. Imputation using mean substitution. 

btdiversityz
Business Type Diversity Z-score. Calculated as the number of different business types within a group divided by the number of group members. The values are normalized with a mean of zero and a standard deviation of one. 

propbtpettyi
Proportion of group members engaged in petty trading. Imputation using mean substitution. 

propbtpetty
Proportion of group members engaged in petty trading. 

propbtclothi
Proportion of group members engaged in clothing sales. Imputation using mean substitution. 

propbtcloth
Proportion of group members engaged in clothing sales. 

propbtfoodi
Proportion of group members engaged in food services. Imputation using mean substitution. 

propbtfood 
Proportion of group members engaged in food services. 

propbtitemi 
Proportion of group members engaged in single item trading. Imputation using mean substitution.

propbtitem 
Proportion of group members engaged in single item trading.

propbtservicei 
Proportion of group members engaged in services. Imputation using mean substitution. 

propbtservice 
Proportion of group members engaged in services. 

groupid 
Group identifier

branchid 
Identifier for branch of the lending institution that issued the loan and where repayments are made.

officerid 
Identifier for each loan officer.  

city_main 
Identifier for the city in which the loan was taken. 

scheduled_running_month 
Month in which the payment is due, as a running variable so that values do not reset at the start of a new year.

time_span_division
Indicator that divides the time span of dataset into 3 mutually exclusive periods based on the lending agency's volume of loans

moves_to_cycle_6
Dummy variable for whether the group continues to loan cycle #6.
